Score Format

        <?xml version="1.0" encoding="utf-8"?>
        <score bpm="400" beatspermeasure="4">
	        <instrument instrument="Subtractive">

                <!-- Doe a dear a a female deer -->
                <note measure="1" beat="1" duration="3" note="C4" type="Square"/>
                <note measure="1" beat="4" duration="1" note="D4" type="Square"/>

                <note measure="2" beat="1" duration="3" note="E4" type="Triangle"/>
                <note measure="2" beat="4" duration="1" note="C4" type="Triangle"/>
            </instrument>
            <!-- EFFECTS -->
            <instrument instrument="Reverb">
                    <note measure="1" delay=".0025" wet="1" dry="1" reverbTime="0.5" />
                    <note measure="2" delay=".0025" wet="1" dry="1" reverbTime="0.7" />
            </instrument>
            </score>

Here's a sample score file. First there is an instrument subtractive being played at measure 1 and 2. Next there is a reverb instrument that will be played as an effect. It will also be played at measure 1 and 2 So in when the synthesizer reads in the file, at measure 1 and 2 the synthesizer will generate Subtractive based on the attributes in the notes tag and it will apply the reverb effect to the generated frame.
